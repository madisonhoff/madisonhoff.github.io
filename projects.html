The Brown and White associate data and graphics editor

I was an associate data and graphics editor for Lehigh University's newspaper for two semesters. During this time, I created different data visualizations to go along with articles.
I made a line chart of the unemployment rate in the different areas of the Lehigh Valley over the years and a bar chart to show the unemployment rate in the Lehigh Valley in 2017. These charts went along with another reporter's article to communicate the unemployment rate problem in a simple way of charts. I created the charts in Tableau Public and Piktochart.

Click here to see my two graphics on Lehigh Valley unemployment rate in 2017 and over time.
I made two bar charts for Lehigh women's soccer and women's basketball for an article about how many athletes made honor roll. Readers could click what year they wanted to see the number of students who were on the honor roll and compare to other years. I created the charts using Patriot League data in Tableau Public and Piktochart.

Interactive visualization

Putting data on a chart may not be too helpful to a viewer. They may want to look at a specific data point or to focus on one variable. Interactivity within the data visualization is therefore important. I created an interactive line chart about Lehigh graduation rates by ethnicity and race. To create this visualization, I used Tableau Public , which allows users to create interactive visualizations for free with an account.
